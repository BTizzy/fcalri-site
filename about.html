Our Story

Three decades of family-style care in Bristol.

Franklin Court has been a small, family-run assisted living community since 1993 — proudly operated by East Bay Community Development Corp, a Bristol-based nonprofit serving the East Bay since 1969.

Our Mission

To serve every resident with the utmost care, respect, and dignity — while preserving their independence.

This is also true of our coworkers, family members, vendors and visitors. Everything we do — from the way we greet residents in the morning to how we plan activities for the week — starts from this simple principle.

We hold ourselves to this standard because we know that choosing an assisted living community is one of the most consequential decisions a family will make. The right home does not just provide care — it preserves identity, dignity, and joy.

Our Philosophy

Small, by design.

We keep our community intentionally small. With about 30 residents and a longstanding care team, every staff member knows every resident by name, by routine, by family. There is no rotating door of unfamiliar faces here.

That scale also shapes how we deliver care. Medication management, help with bathing and dressing, weekly housekeeping, three daily meals, and round-the-clock nursing are not add-ons to a base rate. They are included — because the things that make a residence feel like home shouldn't carry a surcharge.

Our Parent Nonprofit

Part of something larger.

Franklin Court is operated by East Bay Community Development Corp (EBCDC), a Bristol-based 501(c)(3) nonprofit serving low- and moderate-income residents of the East Bay since 1969.

Founded 1969

East Bay CDC

A nonprofit community development corporation headquartered in Bristol, serving families and seniors throughout the East Bay region of Rhode Island.

Mission-driven

Nonprofit operation

Surplus revenue reinvested into resident care, facility improvements, and outreach — not distributed to shareholders.

Vetted

State-licensed

Licensed by the Rhode Island Department of Health, regularly inspected, and held to state standards for assisted living residences.
