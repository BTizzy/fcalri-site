Life at Franklin Court

A neighborhood, not a campus.

We're housed in the award-winning Kaiser Mill complex — a thoughtfully restored textile mill in Bristol's historic district. The result is something rare in assisted living: rooms with character, original mill windows, and a courtyard anchored by a 19th-century brick walkway.

Step outside and you're a five-minute walk from the heart of Bristol — Hope Street, the harbor, the Independence Park waterfront, and the East Bay Bike Path.

What you'll find here

Spaces designed for real life.

Every common space at Franklin Court is sized for connection — large enough to feel like a community, small enough to feel like home.

Dining

Main dining room

Overlooking the open-air courtyard. Full-service dining three times a day, plus private dining room for family celebrations.

Library

Formal library

A quiet reading room with curated books, comfortable seating, and a different kind of stillness than a phone screen.

Events

East Bay Community Room

Our largest gathering space — used for holiday parties, exercise classes, social events, and live performances.

Cafe

Mt. Hope Cafe & Game Room

A charming spot for cards, board games, casual conversation, and a mid-morning coffee.

Wellness

Wellness Center

On-site wellness services and clinic coordination — without leaving the building.

Outdoors

Enclosed courtyard

Walking paths, patio seating, raised garden beds, and space for cookouts. Fully enclosed for safety.

All-inclusive

Everything you need. One monthly fee.

The first thing most families notice about Franklin Court is what isn't on our invoice. There are no tiered packages. There are no surprise add-ons for medication management or personal care. There is one monthly fee, and it includes:

  • Three fresh meals a day, plus snacks
  • Medication management & pharmacy coordination
  • Assistance with bathing, dressing, and grooming
  • Weekly housekeeping and personal laundry
  • Help coordinating transportation to appointments
  • 24-hour personal emergency call response
  • Daily activities programming & weekly outings
  • All utilities including cable TV, telephone, and internet
  • Mailboxes, maintenance, and grounds-keeping
A Day in the Life

What a Tuesday looks like.

Resident routines vary, and our programming adapts around them. But here's roughly what a typical day might look like.

  • 7:30 AMWake at your own pace. Help with morning routine as needed.
  • 8:30 AMBreakfast in the main dining room — coffee, eggs to order, fruit.
  • 10:00 AMMorning activity: exercise class, walking group, manicures, or worship.
  • 12:00 PMLunch. Cards or quiet time after.
  • 2:00 PMAfternoon program — bingo, left-right-center, pokeno, music, or a planned outing.
  • 5:00 PMDinner. After, time in parlor, media room, or just visiting.
  • EveningOn your schedule. Nursing on-site all night if you need anything.
